Welcome to Copper Heart.

We all know people who are musicians, artists, organizers, programmers or writers. And we want to help, to contribute to their cause.

Copper Heart makes patronage easy. It's is a way for communities to support people doing good work.

Donating is a personal act, yet most of the time – on the web – our only option is a Paypal button to donate now. What does that even mean? How much do these people need, and where does the money go?

Kickstarter and Indiegogo are wonderful, giving hundreds of millions of dollars to our friends, to strangers, for a chance to work on their dreams, or at least a cool project. But what happens after that project is complete?

Backing a project is about helping the people behind a cause, helping them put a roof over their heads, helping them relax after a worthwhile day. It's about helping them know a community has their backs, that we're in this together.

Copper Heart is a way to make monthly contributions to people you know, using your credit card.

About 97 percent of each contribution goes directly to the person you're supporting. The other 3 percent goes to Stripe, for handling confidential data and processing cards.

Anyone in the United States or Canada can contribute.

You can set up your own Copper Heart in about a day. The code for this project is freely available on GitHub.
